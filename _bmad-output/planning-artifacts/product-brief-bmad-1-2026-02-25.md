# Product Brief: bmad-1

## Executive Summary

Agun Gunawan is a Senior Frontend Developer with 8+ years of hands-on experience
building modern, high-quality web interfaces using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React JS,
and Webflow. This personal portfolio website serves as the primary professional
presence for Agun — a single, compelling destination where startups, software houses,
and companies can discover his expertise, review his work, and initiate meaningful
engagement with confidence.

The site is built from scratch to strike the right balance: approachable and
collaborative in tone, yet undeniably professional and trustworthy in execution.
It removes friction between a potential client discovering Agun and deciding to
reach out — converting visitors into qualified leads across freelance, part-time,
and full-time opportunities.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Senior frontend developers with deep expertise are difficult to evaluate online.
Generic job boards and crowded platforms make it hard for quality talent to stand
out — and equally hard for clients to find and trust the right person for their
project. Agun currently relies on LinkedIn and email, but lacks a dedicated,
owned professional presence that tells his full story on his own terms.

### Problem Impact

Without a compelling personal website:

- Potential clients cannot quickly validate Agun's skills, style, and credibility
- Opportunities are lost to developers with stronger online presence
- The discovery-to-contact journey is fragmented and lacks a professional anchor
- Agun's 8+ years of experience and unique Webflow + React expertise go underrepresented

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- **LinkedIn**: Noisy, competitive, not fully in Agun's control — hard to differentiate
- **Job Boards (Upwork, Toptal)**: Commoditize talent, invite price competition, wrong audience
- **Generic Portfolio Templates**: Lack personality, don't communicate trust or collaboration
- None of these create an owned, always-on professional brand experience

### Proposed Solution

A purpose-built personal portfolio website for Agun Gunawan that:

- Introduces Agun with warmth, credibility, and clarity from the first scroll
- Showcases curated projects with context (problem → solution → outcome)
- Communicates availability and openness to freelance, part-time, and full-time roles
- Makes it effortless to reach out via a contact form, email, or LinkedIn
- Reflects an approachable, collaborative, and trusted professional brand

### Key Differentiators

- **Owned Channel**: Fully controlled, no algorithm dependency
- **Dual-Stack Credibility**: Rare combination of React JS (engineering depth) + Webflow
  (no-code speed) appeals to both technical and non-technical decision-makers
- **Human-First Tone**: Not just a resume — a window into how Agun thinks and works
- **Trust by Design**: Professional aesthetic backed by real social proof
  (testimonials, case studies, availability status)
- **8+ Years Signal**: Experience level immediately clear, filtering for quality opportunities

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### Persona 1: "The Tech Recruiter" — Sarah, Independent/Agency Recruiter

- **Role:** Talent acquisition specialist at a recruitment agency or in-house recruiter at a tech company
- **Context:** Actively sourcing senior frontend developers for client companies or internal teams; browses 10–20 developer profiles per day
- **Goals:** Quickly validate skills, assess cultural fit, confirm availability, and initiate contact
- **Pain Points:** Most developer profiles are technical walls of text — hard to gauge personality or communication style; unclear availability status wastes her time
- **What She Needs on the Site:** Clear skill summary, years of experience upfront, availability indicator, easy contact method, and a sense of who Agun is as a person
- **Aha Moment:** "This person is experienced, looks easy to work with, and has a contact form — let me reach out today."

#### Persona 2: "The Startup CTO/Founder" — Budi, Early-Stage Startup Founder

- **Role:** Technical co-founder or CTO at a startup (5–50 people), often wearing multiple hats
- **Context:** Looking for a reliable senior frontend dev for a short-term project or ongoing part-time engagement; values speed and quality over process
- **Goals:** Find someone who can hit the ground running with React; verify past work quality; gauge if they'll be easy to collaborate with
- **Pain Points:** Job boards are flooded with juniors and mismatched profiles; doesn't want to go through a recruiter and pay fees; needs trust signals fast
- **What He Needs on the Site:** Portfolio projects with real context (stack used, problem solved, outcome), testimonials, and a frictionless way to start a conversation
- **Aha Moment:** "He built something very similar to what we need — and the work looks clean. I'll drop him a message."

#### Persona 3: "The HR Manager at a Software House" — Diana, HR Manager

- **Role:** HR or talent manager at a software house or digital agency (20–200 people)
- **Context:** Has a recurring need for frontend talent; looking for someone who could work remotely on client projects part-time or full-time
- **Goals:** Assess professionalism, communication, and technical fit; gather enough info to present to the technical team
- **Pain Points:** LinkedIn profiles feel transactional; hard to get a sense of real quality or working style from a resume alone
- **What She Needs on the Site:** Professional impression, clear skill stack, downloadable CV option, and a contact form that doesn't feel like shouting into a void
- **Aha Moment:** "This site feels professional, not just another template — I'm going to forward this to our tech lead."

Agun's dual React + Webflow expertise makes him uniquely compelling to this segment.

### User Journey

**Discovery → Evaluation → Contact**

1. **Discovery:** Finds Agun via LinkedIn, Google search, referral, or job post
2. **Landing:** Arrives on homepage — immediately sees name, title, and a clear value statement
3. **Evaluation:** Scans skills & experience → reviews 2–3 portfolio projects → reads a testimonial or two
4. **Trust Build:** Notes 8+ years experience, sees familiar tech stack, feels the professional-yet-human tone
5. **Decision Point:** Checks availability status → clicks contact or copies email
6. **Contact:** Sends inquiry via contact form or LinkedIn → Agun follows up

## Success Metrics

The primary goal of the portfolio site is to convert visitors into qualified leads
by enabling two core actions: reviewing completed projects and downloading Agun's CV —
ultimately driving contact via the built-in email form.

### User Success Metrics

- **CV Downloads:** Visitor successfully finds and downloads Agun's CV within the session
- **Portfolio Engagement:** Visitor reviews at least 2–3 completed project case studies
- **Contact Initiated:** Visitor submits the contact form after reviewing work and/or CV
- **Clarity of Value:** Visitor can immediately identify Agun's skills, experience level,
  and availability within the first scroll — no hunting required

### Business Objectives

| Objective           | Target                                                         | Timeframe                              |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Site Launch         | Live and fully functional                                      | Within 2 months (by end of April 2026) |

## MVP Scope

The following features constitute the Minimum Viable Product, targeting a
launch within 2 months (by end of April 2026):

### Core Features

1. **Homepage / Hero Section**
   - Name, title ("Senior Frontend Developer"), and a compelling value statement
   - "Open to Work" availability badge (visible, prominent)
   - CTA buttons: View Projects, Download CV, Contact Me

2. **About Me Section**
   - Professional background and personal story
   - 8+ years experience highlight
   - Core skills: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React JS, Webflow, Next.js

3. **Portfolio / Projects Section**
   - Project Cards format: thumbnail image + project title + tech stack used + live link/GitHub
   - Curated selection of best completed work
   - Filter by technology (optional but lightweight)

4. **CV Download**
   - Prominent, accessible download button
   - PDF format, always up to date

5. **Contact Form**
   - Email-based contact form (name, email, subject, message)
   - Direct delivery to Agun's inbox
   - Confirmation message after submission

6. **Footer**
   - Links to LinkedIn and email
   - Copyright

**Tech Stack:**

- **Framework:** Next.js (React-based, SSG/SSR for SEO performance)
- **Language:** English (targeting global visitors)
- **Hosting:** TBD (Vercel recommended for Next.js)
